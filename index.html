What we do

CSIP is a Princeton student organization founded in 2017! Our mission is to empower
students to support social enterprises striving to create a better China by:

I. Empowering students with the knowledge and tools they need to evaluate and
provide project-based support to our partner organizations.

II. Providing a forum on campus for learning about social entrepreneurship and
social-profit organizations in China

III. Raising an Impact Fund which CSIP students will donate to one of our partner organizations.

CSIP Program 2017 - 2018

The CSIP Program gives students the opportunity to engage with the fast developing social
sector in China and Hong Kong.Our program this year runs from July 2017 to May 2018.

From July to August, students enroll in an online course on social impact and organization
evaluation, which was developed and provided to us by Philanthropy in Motion, our strategic
partner in China. We place students in teams of three and match them with one of our five
partner social enterprises for the year. The teams work closely with these enterprises to
gain a deep understanding of their business, and ultimately present their learnings in an evaluation
report at the end of the online course. Several of our partner organizations are emerging
enterprises that lack capacity in areas where students can provide valuable support. During the fall
and spring partner organizations, contributing their skills while gaining deep insight into
how these organizations are creating change. Example projects include conducting
market research, case studies,and basic financial modeling.

Philanthropy in Motion


PIM and its “learn by giving” approach empowers millenials with the funding, training, and networks to take a strategic, venture capitalist model to philanthropy and decide how to best allocate funds and resources to impactful social ventures. Students are guided through a comprehensive process of identifying their missions, investigating social issues, and leveraging collective resources to advance social innovation in China.

SmartAir


A social enterprise that promotes DIY air filters as a low cost solution to indoor particulate air pollution in China, India, and other countries. It’s also dedicated to educating the population about the importance of clean air, through providing DIY educational workshops in cities across China.

Farm to Neighbors


A pioneering sustainable and locally sourced farmers market in Beijing, dedicated to helping people in China enjoy healthy and organic food. The company is also working to help people re-evaluate their diets as a gateway to long-term physical health.

Diinsider


A social enterprise dedicated to creating changes in the grassroots level, by cpnnecting social impact organizations with intellectual resources, interntational talents and impact investors. It also assists impact investors to identify outstanding practices of social innovations and make good decisions to support the grassroots.

MyH2O


A crowd based data platform on rural drinking water and sanitation conditions in China. The company provides students with the resources to conduct general assessments of local demands on clean water and sanitation. Its goal is for people to subscribe to the platform and to connect needs with services.
