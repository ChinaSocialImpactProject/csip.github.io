What we do

CSIP is a Princeton student organization founded in 2017!
Our mission is to empower students to support social
enterprises striving to create a better China by:

I. Empowering students with the knowledge and tools they
need to evaluate and provide project-based support to our
partner organizations.

II. Providing a forum on campus for learning about social
entrepreneurship and social-profit organizations in China

III. Raising an Impact Fund which CSIP students will donate
to one of our partner organizations.

CSIP Program 2017 - 2018

The CSIP Program gives students the opportunity to
engage with the fast developing social sector in China
and Hong Kong.Our program this year runs from
July 2017 to May 2018.

From July to August, students enroll in an online
course on social impact and organization evaluation,
which was developed and provided to us by
Philanthropy in Motion, our strategic partner in
China. We place students in teams of three and
match them with one of our five partner social
enterprises for the year. The teams work closely
with these enterprises to gain a deep understanding
of their business, and ultimately present their
learnings in an evaluation report at the end of
the online course. Several of our partner
organizations are emerging enterprises that
lack capacity in areas where students can provide
valuable support. During the fall and spring
semesters, students work on projects for the
partner organizations, contributing their skills
while gaining deep insight into how these
organizations are creating change. Example
projects include conducting market research,
case studies,and basic financial modeling.

Board

Vera Lummis

Vera is a senior in the Woodrow Wilson School and in the Entrepreneurship Certificate Program, and is interested in pursuing a career in the intersection of international development and business. After participating in Philanthropy in Motion's Model Foundation Program in summer of 2016, she became inspired to bring education around social entrepreneurship and China's social sector development to students at Princeton. As the founder of CSIP, she is hoping that students will through the program become passionate about social change in China and the implications of social entrepreneurship for global sustainable development. For her senior thesis she is researching the implications of the new Foreign NGO Law in China on civil society development and rule of law, and is excited to see how her research might form a basis for future educational initiatives organized by CSIP.

Oliver Hsu

Oliver is a junior in the Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs at Princeton, focusing on technology policy and development. On campus, he has helped lead a number of social impact organizations, such as Princeton Social Innovation and the Pace Council for Civic Values. He is also an officer of the rugby team and a Peer Academic Advisor. Oliver was introduced to social entrepreneurship through being involved in a charity startup back in high school, and hopes to replicate his experience for others by building a scalable model of social entrepreneurship education through CSIP. His other interests include startups, venture capital, travel, and endurance sports.

Nick Wu

Nick Wu is a junior concentrating in the Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs, and is pursuing a certificate in Computer Science. Nick is from Bucks County, Pennsylvania near Philadelphia, and got interested in China’s social sector after he spent a summer in Beijing through Princeton in Beijing, which opened his eyes to the intersection of the social impact/non-profit world and China. Nick has learned a lot from the fellow cofounders of CSIP, and hopes that CSIP can continue to link interested college students to aspiring social enterprises in China, and looks forward to working closely with the rest of the team and the participants on making their experience worthwhile.

William Hinthorn

William is a senior CS student researching neural representations of faces with the Visual AI lab. He plans on creating an ML-powered education or health organization to restore the dream of the internet’s creators to bring about global democratization of information and public empowerment. Will previously researched visual perception algorithms with the Beijing-based self-driving car company, Momenta and studied history and tech courses at Tsinghua University. Will hopes that CSIP will be a catalyst in enabling students to make the leap into social entrepreneurship and start early in building and innovating for the public good.

Katie Zhou

After her recent PICS internship at YingHua International School where she did business development and marketing work, Katie is especially excited to work with CSIP to create marketing content, work with the leadership team, and overall get more of the Princeton community aware of and passionate about the social entrepreneurship sector in China as well as the work that CSIP is doing.

Hanseul Nam

Hanseul is a junior in the Economics department, minoring in Applied Math and Computer Science. Her interests include tech, entrepreneurship and philosophy, and she is currently examining the economic implications of cryptocurrencies/blockchain technologies in her independent work. She's excited by the potential of innovation and technology to provide rapid, large-scale solutions to some of our most pressing & systemic problems. Smart Air Filters

Smart Air China is a social enterprise that promotes low cost DIY air filters as a low cost solution to indoor particulate air pollution in China, India, Mongolia and other countries. It is also dedicated to educating the population about the importance of clean air, through providing DIY educational workshops in cities across China.
